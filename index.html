    About Me

    Career

    My journey in technology began with a focus on video game programming, where I developed an educational repository for learning ray tracing with Vulkan as my thesis. Over the years, I have expanded my knowledge and skills to include cybersecurity, gaining a comprehensive and in-depth understanding of the field.

I've designed and held Capture the Flag (CTF) competitions, created programming materials to support artificial intelligence and video game programming courses, and am assisting in the implementation of a specialized lab for CCNA7 training. Additionally, I manage and maintain campus laboratories, I've developed introductory courses for ethical hacking, and worked in redesigning the academic program for the cybersecurity career. Additionally, I've offered training for students for the Certified Entry-Level Python Programmer (PCEP) certification for the Continuous Education department.

My responsibilities included resolving Tier 2 support issues and aiding in the implementation of the company's cloud service for their second biggest client in Mexico, which was also the company's biggest project that year. Additionally, I documented all of the more challenging issues that lacked any sort of playbook on how to resolve.

    My professional journey has been driven by a deep curiosity and passion for technology. Initially drawn to video game programming, I found my interests expanding towards cybersecurity. The dynamic and critical nature of this field captivated me, leading me to make a deliberate shift towards it. This transition was fueled by a desire to not only protect and secure digital environments but also to share this crucial knowledge with others. I am committed to fostering a culture of continuous learning and improvement, ensuring that both I and my peers remain at the forefront of technological advancements.

    Looking ahead, I am eager to further immerse myself in the cybersecurity field, seeking out new challenges and opportunities for collaboration. I look forward to connecting with other professionals, exchanging ideas, and working together to address the evolving threats in cybersecurity.
